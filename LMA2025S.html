Course Information

Course Description

Large Language Models (LLMs) are transforming how tasks are automated and personalized across domains. As LLMs evolve into interactive agents, they are set to drive the next major breakthrough in AI. This course introduces the foundations of LLMs, key capabilities for task automation, and essential infrastructures for building LLM agents. We will explore applications such as generation, tool augmentation, web automation, healthcare, and scientific research, while also addressing current limitations and future directions.

Students will learn the theoretical foundations of transformer architectures, attention mechanisms, and modern training techniques including pre-training, fine-tuning, and reinforcement learning from human feedback. The course also covers AI agents, multi-agent systems, tool use, reasoning capabilities, and real-world applications. Students will implement key algorithms and build practical systems throughout the course.

Prerequisites

Students should have completed at least one graduate-level introductory course in AI/ML, such as Introduction to NLP, Statistical Methods in AI, Advanced NLP, or Information Retrieval and Extraction.

Late Policy

  • Assignments submitted late will be penalized 10% per day (including weekends)
  • No assignments will be accepted more than 3 days late
  • Each student has one "grace period" of 48 hours that can be used once during the semester
  • Extensions may be granted for documented emergencies or health issues

Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be your own. Plagiarism, copying from online sources, or unauthorized collaboration will result in a failing grade for the assignment and may result in a failing grade for the course.

You may use AI tools (like ChatGPT, Copilot) for learning and understanding concepts, but not for generating solutions to assignments. Any use of AI tools must be disclosed in your submission.
